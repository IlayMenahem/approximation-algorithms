\subsection*{The question - The bus network problem}
Let $G = (V, E)$ be a directed graph, B the number of buses, a metric
$t: V \times V \to \mathbb{R}_+$, and a set of $n$ passengers, where
each passenger $i$ is a tuple of the form $(s_i, d_i, t_i)$, where $s_i$ is the
source vertex, $d_i$ is the destination vertex, and $t_i$ is the time at which
the passenger wants to arrive at the destination. The bus network problem is
to find a schedule for the buses, such that each bus starts at some vertex at
time 0, and travels along a path in the graph, and each passenger can pick any
bus that passes through $s_i$ at some time and arrives at $d_i$ at time before
$t_i$. The objective is to minimize the total difference between the time the
started at the source and the desired arrival time.
